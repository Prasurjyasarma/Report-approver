
SAFE CLAIMS ADJUDICATION POLICY – POC VERSION
(Adapted from Healthcare Claims & Adjudication Standards)

---------------------------------------------------------------------
1. PURPOSE
---------------------------------------------------------------------
This document defines simplified but realistic policy rules for
automated healthcare claim adjudication within the Safe Claims
Agentic AI Proof-of-Concept system.

These policies are inspired by healthcare payer adjudication
standards and define how claims should be validated, reviewed,
and reimbursed by an automated claims agent.

The purpose of these rules is to enable an AI system to:
• ingest claim data
• interpret coverage rules
• validate coding standards
• detect anomalies and fraud
• determine claim outcomes

These rules simulate the behavior of real insurance claim
adjudication engines used by healthcare payers.

---------------------------------------------------------------------
2. CLAIM DATA REQUIREMENTS
---------------------------------------------------------------------
Each claim submitted to the Safe Claims system must include
the following mandatory information:

• Claim ID
• Patient Identifier
• Provider Identifier
• Date of Service
• Diagnosis Code (ICD-10)
• Procedure Code (CPT / HCPCS)
• Billed Amount

If any required field is missing, the claim must be flagged
for manual review.

Example Outcome:
Decision: PENDING
Reason: Missing required claim data

---------------------------------------------------------------------
3. ELIGIBILITY VERIFICATION
---------------------------------------------------------------------
Prior to adjudicating any claim, the system must verify
that the patient was eligible for coverage on the date of service.

Eligibility checks include:
• Active insurance policy
• Valid coverage period
• Member enrollment status

If a member is not eligible on the date of service:

Decision: REJECTED
Reason: Member not eligible for coverage

---------------------------------------------------------------------
4. MEDICAL CODING REQUIREMENTS
---------------------------------------------------------------------
All healthcare claims must use standardized medical coding systems.

Accepted code systems include:

• ICD‑10‑CM – Diagnosis classification
• CPT – Medical procedures
• HCPCS – Healthcare services and supplies

Claims containing invalid, outdated, or unsupported codes
must be rejected or returned for correction.

Example:

Decision: REJECTED
Reason: Invalid procedure code

---------------------------------------------------------------------
5. COVERAGE VALIDATION
---------------------------------------------------------------------
Insurance plans define which procedures and services are covered.

When adjudicating a claim, the system must verify that:

• The billed procedure is included in the policy benefits
• The service category is covered
• The service meets plan limitations

If a procedure is not covered under the member’s plan:

Decision: REJECTED
Reason: Non‑covered service

---------------------------------------------------------------------
6. PRIOR AUTHORIZATION REQUIREMENTS
---------------------------------------------------------------------
Certain services require preauthorization before being performed.

Examples include:
• Advanced imaging procedures
• Surgical procedures
• High‑cost treatments
• Specialty services

If a claim contains a procedure requiring authorization
but no authorization record is found:

Decision: PENDING
Action: Request prior authorization documentation

---------------------------------------------------------------------
7. MEDICAL NECESSITY REVIEW
---------------------------------------------------------------------
Services billed must be medically necessary based on the
patient’s diagnosis and clinical condition.

A procedure should generally correspond to a valid
diagnosis code.

Claims may be flagged if:
• procedure does not align with diagnosis
• treatment frequency is clinically unreasonable
• services exceed expected utilization

Outcome:
Decision: FLAGGED
Reason: Medical necessity review required

---------------------------------------------------------------------
8. MEDICALLY UNLIKELY EDITS (MUE)
---------------------------------------------------------------------
Medically Unlikely Edits define the maximum number of
times a procedure can be performed for a patient within
a single day.

Example:

Procedure: Laparoscopic Appendectomy
Maximum Units Allowed: 1 per day

If the claim exceeds the medically allowed frequency:

Decision: REJECTED
Reason: Medically unlikely procedure frequency

These edits are used to prevent billing errors and
detect abnormal utilization patterns.

---------------------------------------------------------------------
9. DUPLICATE CLAIM DETECTION
---------------------------------------------------------------------
Claims must be checked for duplication.

A claim is considered duplicate if another claim exists
with the same:

• Patient ID
• Procedure Code
• Provider
• Date of Service

Duplicate submissions should be rejected.

Decision: REJECTED
Reason: Duplicate claim detected

---------------------------------------------------------------------
10. FRAUD AND ANOMALY DETECTION
---------------------------------------------------------------------
The system should monitor claims for potential fraud
or abnormal billing patterns.

Indicators of suspicious activity include:

• unusually high billed amounts
• repeated procedures within short intervals
• provider billing patterns outside normal ranges
• services inconsistent with provider specialty

When anomalies are detected:

Decision: FLAGGED
Action: Route claim for investigation

---------------------------------------------------------------------
11. REIMBURSEMENT METHODS
---------------------------------------------------------------------
Claims may be reimbursed using several payment methodologies.

Typical reimbursement models include:

Fee‑For‑Service (FFS)
Each service is billed individually based on a
fee schedule.

Per Diem Payment
A fixed daily rate covering services during a
patient stay.

Diagnosis Related Group (DRG)
A bundled payment based on the patient’s
diagnosis and treatment complexity.

The Safe Claims system may simulate these
reimbursement models for decision logic.

---------------------------------------------------------------------
12. CLAIM DECISION OUTCOMES
---------------------------------------------------------------------
After completing all validation checks, the system
assigns a final decision.

Possible outcomes include:

APPROVED
The claim passes all validation checks and
is approved for reimbursement.

REJECTED
The claim violates policy rules or eligibility requirements.

PENDING
Additional information or documentation is required.

FLAGGED
The claim requires manual investigation due
to anomalies or fraud indicators.

---------------------------------------------------------------------
13. AGENTIC CLAIMS PROCESSING WORKFLOW
---------------------------------------------------------------------
Within the Safe Claims agentic system,
claims will be processed using the following workflow:

1. Ingest claim data (EDI claim parsing)
2. Retrieve policy rules and supporting documents
3. Analyze claim context
4. Execute validation checks
5. Detect anomalies
6. Determine claim outcome

This workflow enables autonomous claims adjudication
while adhering to established policy rules.
